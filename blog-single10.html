ENERGY TIPS FOR SMALL BUSINESS START UPS

  • John Doe
  • Jan 1, 2020
  • 12 Comments

Small businesses run up costs in the process of their daily activities. However, some of these expenses, specifically those on energy, can be reduced by following some of these simple tips outlined below.

1. Turn off electrical appliances when they are not in use: Putting them on standby still consumes some amount of energy. The multiplier effect of little consumptions from time to time amounts to huge wastage in the long term.

2. Buy energy efficient appliances: They consume less energy and provide more satisfaction. Look out for the Energy label. The Energy label gives information on how much energy or how efficient the appliance is.

3. Keep refrigerators properly closed except when you need to take/place something out/in respectively. For every time you open the refrigerator, cool air is lost. If the refrigerator is not filled up, bottles of water can be used to fill up the space. This helps to retain the cool air. This also applies to freezers.

4. To save energy consumed by Air Conditioners, make sure windows and doors are properly closed and little openings are sealed. It takes more energy to cool the office-space if there are leakages.

5. Reduce carbon footprint by printing only very important documents. Every other thing should be done via electronic means. Drafts can also be printed on the other side of a paper that has been used on only one side.

6. Boil the right amount of water if you have to drink coffee or tea. There is no point wasting lots of energy to boil a full kettle when you only need a quarter. This is because the remaining three-quarters cools off and will need to be boiled again for use by another person. This will waste energy that would have been used for another productive purpose.

7. Water wastage translates to more energy wastage. Turn on taps only when you need to, especially when washing hands. Leaving the water running means draining the tank and this translates to using energy to replace it. So water should be used wisely.

8. Use more day-lighting during the day in the office space, especially when there is enough day-lighting for tasks to be carried out. Use electric lights only for specific purposes. For example, for a store that is dark or not well lit by day lighting.

9. Locally-made occupancy sensors are available and can be used to regulate the lighting in the office space. This means that if the last employee to leave a particular space forgets to turn off the light, the sensor can turn off the light automatically after a pre-determined number of minutes.

10. Replace incandescent bulbs with energy saving bulbs such as the Compact Fluorescent Lamps and LED bulbs. This saves about 50 – 70% of energy consumed by lighting only. Incandescent bulbs are rated 60W, 100W and 200W while Compact Fluorescent Lamps rated as low as 5W – 25W will give the same or more illumination and better comfort. A typical example is replacing 10 no. 60W incandescent bulbs with 10 no. 5W LED bulbs. This will save 550W just by replacing the incandescent bulbs with LED bulbs.
