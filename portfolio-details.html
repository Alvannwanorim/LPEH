THE ENERGY AMBASSADORS CLUB

WEEK ONE

Lesson:
Introduction to Renewable Energy, Energy Efficiency, Energy Conservation and Water Conservation

Activity:
Identification of different renewable energy sources

Homework:
Scrap book and word puzzle

The members of the Energy Ambassadors Club were taught the following concepts on energy, renewable energy, energy efficiency, energy conservation and water conservation. At the end of the lesson, the pupils were able to clearly understand the difference between renewable energy and non-renewable energy, the types and sources of renewable energy, definition of and difference between energy conservation and energy efficiency and ways in which they can save energy. The pupils were also given scrapbooks, to identify the different sources of renewable energy and Word puzzles as their activity for the day and homework. They were also given registration forms to seek their parents’ consents. In the registration form, the responsibilities of the children and the parents were clearly listed as well as special health considerations.

WEEK TWO


Lesson:
Operation, installation and maintenance of solar home systems.

Activity:
Identification of solar home system components, light bulbs

Homework:
Energy audit of homes

In this week, the members of the Energy Ambassadors Club learnt about the components of solar systems. They also had a demonstration exercise on how to install, operate and maintain a solar home system. They also learnt about the different types of light bulbs – incandescent, fluorescent tubes, compact fluorescent lamps and LED light bulbs and how to identify them. They were also taught about the energy consumption of each of these bulbs. After these, they were taught how to conduct energy audits – which simply entails taking a list of all energy appliances in their class and how long those appliances are usually on. They were given energy audit forms to carry out energy audit of their respective homes as their homework.

WEEK THREE


Lesson:
Energy Labels, Spelling exercise and Review of Energy Audit

Activity:
Identification of energy star logo and energy rating on appliances

Homework:
Ways to save energy in my home and school

In week 3, the energy audit exercises were reviewed and there was a spelling exercise to test their knowledge of basic concepts and terms and how to spell them correctly. The members were also taught about the Energy star logo and why it is an important label in identifying energy efficient appliances. A revision exercise on lessons from the first week was also carried out.

WEEK FOUR


Lesson:
Summary of lessons

Activity:
Revision, Energy Audits and Learning about energy consumption of different appliances

Wrap-up:
Radio show at UNILAG 103.1 FM, Induction into Energy Ambassadors Club, Presentation of gifts.

In the fourth week, energy audit was taught expensively using demonstrations with light bulbs, electric kettles and electric irons and how to identify their ratings on the body of any appliance. Revision exercise was also carried out. Photographs were also taken. Update of member data base was also carried out. Selection and Rehearsal of representatives of the Energy Ambassadors Club for the radio program also took place. The selected members were Onyema Chukwuemeka, Brambifa Immanuella and Adekunle-Segun Iyanuoluwa.

SUMMARY

La-Peonular Energy Hub Ltd with the support of the management and teachers of the University of Lagos Staff School during the Summer School established the Energy Ambassadors Club with the aim to support the National level campaign on energy conservation, energy efficiency and renewable energy in Nigerian schools and as well the Sustainable Development Goals 7. Engaging activities, home works and hands on experiences were used mostly to help the children understand all the concepts taught. Some representatives of the Club also educated the public at the end of their Club activities on the UNILAG Radio 103.1FM. All the objectives highlighted in the concept note were achieved. The Energy Ambassadors Club now has 38 members and we look forward to having a network of 1 million members across Nigeria.
