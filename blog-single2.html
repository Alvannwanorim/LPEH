Electricity Tariff Increase: Why metering should come first

  • @naijaenergyman
  • Jan 1, 2020

The recent increase in electricity tariff by 40% has caused several reactions from Nigerians in all sectors of the economy. The Minister of Power, Works and Housing, Babatunde Raji Fashola (SAN) is of the opinion that this move is here to serve Nigerians better, while many business owners and consumers on estimated billings system are of the opinion that the quality of service is of utmost importance. I personally have had a lot of these discussions with many professionals in the industry across Africa, even to the extent of social media debates.

DISCOs are in business to provide energy services whilst also trying to break even. One of their responsibilities is to provide prepaid meters to their customers.

A sample survey was carried out to buttress a point that will be made later in the article. Out of 225 respondents asked about cost of the telecommunication services they use; only 3 knew the actual tariff in kobo/sec of their respective telecommunication service providers. Respondents generally claim that they trust the billing system and so they recharge their phones because that’s the only way they can access services such as data, calls and SMS. So if the tariff for calls went from 40k/sec to 52k/sec for example, the customers will rarely notice and since there is a feedback mechanism to check how much credit in naira they have left, then this informs their subsequent decisions of who to call and how long to call until when they would recharge next. I doubt if anyone calls the telecommunications customer care that they noticed an increase in tariff from 40k/sec to 52k/sec. Nobody does that.

Now, back to the increase in tariff, the DISCOs have a responsibility to the customer and vice versa. The DISCOs are in business to provide energy services whilst also trying to break even. One of their responsibilities is to provide prepaid meters to their customers. In many cases, customers pay for this meters which are meant to be free and still do not get the meters.

On the other hand, it is the responsibility of consumers to pay their bills. YES, there are issues of meters being bypassed, especially with the users of the traditional “old black analog meters” which are supposed to be replaced with prepaid meters. The new prepaid meters installed are a very welcome idea and the focus of the DISCOs, the regulatory body, NERC, as well as the Ministry of Power, is to increase penetration of these prepaid meters. Because just like in the telecommunications analogy, the prepaid meters need to come first because the customers know it is automated and they can trust it. Then we can begin to talk of increase in tariffs. If tariff is increased and Nigerians are metered, then they will learn to adjust their consumption pattern since it is a “pay as you consume” system just like in our telecommunications analogy.

ISO 50007 which focuses on the activities relating to energy services – guidelines for the assessment and improvement of the service to users should be adopted by the NERC to protect the energy users in the country and of course there should be stiffer penalties for both energy service providers and energy users who default in their duties to the other party.
