Energizing West Africa through Women Education and Empowerment

  • @naijaenergyman
  • Jan 1, 2020

From the historical cultural perspective, women have always been perceived as the weaker gender and this has extended sadly over the years to the educational sectors especially the Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM) related subjects. This is not particularly the fault of the females as this has been culturally ingrained over time giving them the impression that their basic function is housekeeping and raising children, any other thing being an extra activity.

most female children are usually provided with games and items that do not stimulate technological insights, for example, barbie dolls as against the male

Formal education was even considered wasteful since females would eventually be married off to become housewives. In our modern day and with technological advancement, most female children are usually provided with games and items that do not stimulate technological insights, for example, barbie dolls as against the male children with car racing games and brick building. The assumptions that the society makes about males and females, about some perceived differences in their abilities, behaviour, roles and aspiration is also a contributing factor. According to a World Bank report on the Nigerian University System, female academic staff stagnated at a level of about 14 percent. 40 percent earn doctorates in Life Sciences, 20 percent in physical sciences and mathematics and 10percent in Engineering. The female folk have however gladly taken to secretarial, banking and administrative duties and have created a healthy niche there.

For example, there has never been a female Minister of power in Nigeria but there has been a female Minister of Finance. There are only a handful number of female professors in STEM and a few females studying STEM related courses in Higher Institution despite the fact that women make up a higher percentage of the overall population. It is noteworthy to say that over time, women have accepted their position and role in the pyramid. Is it that they are not capable? Is it that their brains are not fully developed? I beg to answer No, because interestingly, the few females that have ventured into STEM related courses and careers have performed excellently well. Some other issues include sexual harassment, financial constraints and domestic related problems such as domestic responsibilities, marriage and business. It also widely acclaimed that STEM courses require full commitment and concentration, hence combining it with domestic duties makes studying a daunting task. Hence, the major issue is the general misconception that women are second place after men and that there are a certain area of knowledge meant for men alone.

Ways in which this issue can be addressed include: To ensure that females are encouraged and given attractive incentives such as scholarships and award of bursaries to participate and study in STEM related courses in both secondary and higher institution levels. A deliberate admission quota for females in STEM related courses and assignment of the female students to female achievers in STEM careers who will serve as mentors. Funds should be made available to provide affordable loans for Small to Medium Enterprises (SMEs) run by female entrepreneurs doing business in the STEM sector. This is very necessary to boost their confidence. Success stories of female achievers in STEM should be published on social media and in the National dailies to encourage younger females. Networks for female engineers and energy enthusiasts should be encouraged to boost professionalism. Vocational training schools strictly for females and fully sponsored should be instituted to enhance skill acquisition and encourage females. Women should also be reoriented about what their role is towards ending poverty and to sustainable development in their communities. They should also be engaged in installation of energy systems and intensive training. Also, STEM based organizations should be enforced to employ a certain percentage of females, if possible, immediate employment, with incentives for the company such as tax rebates or penalties such as heavy fines or whatever needs to be done to ensure smooth compliance. Toys for females should be modified to increase their mental and spatial abilities. Last but not the least, the society at large has to be enlightened on the need for a change of attitude to gender roles.

The likely outcome is that this will encourage more females to engage in more STEM related studies and knowledge acquisition especially in the technical aspects of the energy sector which in turn will serve as a stepping stone for others. This would lead to more female entrepreneurs who will be able to set up small businesses and create employment for other women in engineering activities, in awareness creation, in sales and marketing of technological products, in solar PV systems for residential and mini grids for rural communities and other productive activities. This will help alleviate energy poverty to a large extent and help grow the economy. This will help provide electricity for basic amenities like lighting, clean water and to power small appliances for hair salons, restaurants, schools, markets and hospitals. This also means income for the females and better livelihood for their families. Lastly, the perception of the society will change and it will be accepted and normal for females to venture into the STEM related courses and careers.

The recommended solution is to have programmes and vocational trainings tailored towards developing skill and technical expertise in energy related studies, careers and activities for women. More sponsorship or investment should be made towards women empowerment in the STEM sectors, affordable loans for the creation of small businesses and more employment for women in the STEM sector. Networks for female STEM members should be encouraged to boost professionalism.

The justification is that women have always performed well in hands-on activities such as tailoring, knitting, cooking as well as other STEM related job functions like lab attendants, midwives where a certain level of mastery is usually vital. This implies women can equally perform well in the technical aspects of the energy sector including the innovation, design, manufacture and installation of energy systems and solutions if encouraged. In summary, it is safe to say that women have the capability and mental capacity to be effectively empowered to take on the numerous opportunities available in the energy sector.
