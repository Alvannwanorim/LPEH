Switch Nigeria: from inefficient to efficient

  • John Doe
  • Jan 1, 2020
  • 12 Comments

Nigeria, the most populous black nation in Africa boasts of a population that stands at 170 million people. The giant of Africa as it is fondly called, but giant at what exactly?

While the rest of the world is racing away from inefficient and obsolete technologies, my dear Nigeria still clings tightly like a suckling child deprived for days of its mothers breast.

The incandescent bulbs most commonly used in Nigeria have a power consumption of 60W to 200W. It is used in residential, public, commercial and industrial buildings and applications. It is present in every nook and cranny – the energy vampire always present at every twist of the neck.

It is quite sad that incandescent bulbs still account for about 70% of lighting in Nigeria. Neighbouring Ghana has an import ban on incandescent bulbs while the “giant of Africa” is still clueless as to what you do with these bulbs. Switching from incandescent bulbs to CFLs has a potential saving of between 70 – 80%; to LEDs has a potential saving of between 85 – 95%. This means every 100W incandescent bulb switching to CFL saves an average of 75W, while an LED saves an average of 90W. The multiplier effect of this switch is enormous and gives room for access to power for other productive uses.

Beyond the switch from incandescent bulbs to LEDs, there is also the need to switch from inferior to superior. Simply put, there are many inferior CFL and LEDs bulbs in the Nigerian market. This is one of the reasons why the first switch (Incandescent to CFLs and LEDs) is somewhat difficult. The perception created when substandard CFLs and LEDs fail is one that is hard to erase. It is important therefore that to make Nigeria switch to greatness, it must close its borders to incandescent bulbs and then to inferior and substandard CFLs and LEDs. She must begin to seek ways to manufacture her own CFLs and LEDs to the required standards to meet the lighting demands of her teeming population.

This will create millions of jobs, increase energy access especially to energy poor communities, create economic empowerment, alleviate the burden of energy vampires on the generation, transmission and distribution networks and ensure efficient use of energy.

This is one sure way Nigeria can switch to superior, efficient and most importantly switch to greatness.

Make the switch today.
