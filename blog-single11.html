Energy Conservation through Phantom Loads Elimination

  • @naijaenergyman
  • Jan 1, 2020

When we put off our appliances, we usually think they are off. However in the actual sense, most of them are not “totally off” because they still draw power from the mains. Phantom electrical loads (also known as Vampire electrical loads) refer to appliances which continue to draw power when they are supposedly off.

Any appliance that has a clock, timer, memory or remote control is likely to consume electricity even if it’s turned off

Some examples of Phantom loads include televisions, computers, DVD players, printers, microwave ovens and UPS. A television uses this energy to keep the receiver alert to respond to the remote control. Any appliance that has a clock, timer, memory or remote control is likely to consume electricity even if it’s turned off. I found out that a television consumes 6W, microwave oven 4W and UPS 11W when plugged in but turned off. A wattmeter can be used to measure the amount of power the phantom load draws from the mains. Remember, screen savers in computers are not power savers.

One way to eliminate phantom loads is to unplug the appliance. However, frequent plugging and unplugging can wear out the plug and socket. Cell phone battery chargers and power adapters need to be unplugged when the batteries are full or when they are not in use.

Another way to eliminate phantom loads is to plug the appliance into a power strip (extension lead or surge protector) with an off/on switch. When turned off, the power strip completely cuts off electricity from the appliance.

Also buy appliances with ENERGY STAR® as these appliances consume less energy.

Always remember that it costs far less to save energy than it does to build a power plant to generate energy – apart from the environmental pollution associated with generation using fossil fuel. As the government intensifies effort towards improving power generation, we also ought to conserve the “little” being generated.
