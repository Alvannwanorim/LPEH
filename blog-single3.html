Switch Nigeria – the power of a switch

  • @naijaenergyman
  • Jan 1, 2020

How would you feel if someone turned you on and walked away? This question will most likely be misinterpreted in so many ways but I am glad the message is clear.

It is a pitiful sight to see outdoor lights still on in the blinding brightness of the Nigerian sun. Sometimes I wish a distant wink at these bulbs will make them pop. You do not walk up to a typical Nigerian to turn off their light bulbs in the day time. You will get responses like “Is it your bulb?”, “Are you the one paying the bills?”, “I need it to know when they take and restore power” and if you are unlucky, you may get a beating for not minding your business.

The AC you leave on for hours in your office or home while you drive around the streets; of what use is that AC?

And interestingly, this energy wastage habit knows no creed or tribe – the market woman to the business owner, to public institutions, to religious gatherings, to the learned man. Of what benefit is an appliance that is on without use. The AC you leave on for hours in your office or home while you drive around the streets; of what use is that AC? The lights that are left on, the TV, the running tap; If they are not in use, of what need are they? If a million 11W CFL bulbs are ON in one hour during the day for no specific function or use, 11 MWhr of energy would be wasted.

Can this be used for some other productive use? Of course. It can power an entire hospital with life support facilities; it can power a community, several schools and other beneficial facilities/equipment/machinery.

Always remember, that for every switch, bulb or appliance you put on or off, you have made a decision that may be detrimental or useful to someone in some distant place you may never get to meet. You may save a life or you may take a life, all in a power switch.

Once again, how will you feel if someone turned you ON and walked away?

Please switch wisely.
