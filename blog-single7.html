10 Energy Saving Tips for Easter

  • John Doe
  • Jan 1, 2020
  • 12 Comments

1. Turn off your lights and electrical appliances when they are not in use. Putting them on standby still consumes energy. It is also advisable to use more day lighting if it provides the necessary level of illumination needed in the room. “How would you feel if someone turned you on and walked away?.” Did I just write that? Hmmm

2. Open windows to improve ventilation. This means you may not need to turn on the air conditioner or the fans for long hours and this saves energy. This is only advisable for environments where security is not a concern.

3. Use the right size of pot lids while cooking and the right size of pot on the appropriate burner. A lot of cooking will take place this Easter and this is one way to save on your gas or kerosene or electricity usage.

4. A lot of family and friends will be visiting and there will be a lot of drinking (I mean water o!). Keep freezers and refrigerators properly closed. For every time you open the refrigerator, cool air is lost. It is advisable to be decisive at the refrigerator. No loitering or waiting or thinking while the refrigerator or freezer is open. ‘Money dey waka just like that o’.

5. For those that like eba or tea. Please boil the right amount of water for your various delicacies. Why boil a pot full of water when you need only a quarter to make the eba or drink tea or bathe that little child? The energy used in boiling the wrong amount of water can never be recovered just like Nigeria’s ‘Einstein’ jokingly said “the water used in making eba can never be recovered”.

6. Car-pooling is a good way to save energy. If you have friends, neighbours and family and you all intend to visit the malls or cinemas or supermarket or beach. You can save on fuel consumption by going with 1 car instead of 4. Also, the probability of being stopped by road safety officials becomes lower.

7. Buy locally if you can. Let us patronise our own neighbours and families and friends if they have what we need. This may save you the stress of going far and this saves on fuel consumption too.

8. Sort and dispose waste properly for easy reuse and recycle. All the nylons and shopping bags from the shopping malls can be kept properly for reuse. The plastic can also be sorted for recycling or for reuse.

9. If you still have incandescent bulbs, change them to more energy efficient Compact Fluorescent Lamps and LEDs. This saves about 50 – 70% of energy consumed by lighting only. Incandescent bulbs are rated 60W, 100W and 200W while Compact Fluorescent Lamps rated as low as 5W – 25W will give the same or more illumination and better comfort. A typical example is replacing 10 no. 60W incandescent bulbs with 10 no. 5W LED bulbs. This will save 550W just by replacing the incandescent bulbs with LED bulbs.

10. If you are travelling, remember to gauge your tires and ensure your engine is well serviced. This reduces fuel consumption as well as enhances safety.

Wishing you all a Happy Easter
